## Overview

This project got me into 3D printing. With some leftover money from an old job and good timing with Christmas, I got my hands on a new 3D printer. I was beyond excited — before this, I'd scour the garage for cardboard and wood and make a shoddy attempt at parts like mounts. Now I could use precision printing to do what felt like anything.

I decided to build a model rocket and make all the complementary parts out of PLA. Super fun, and it taught me a ton about CAD and the intricacies of the software.

**Overview.** Build a prototype. I wanted to see if my ideas would work in practice, so I took any junk I could find and tested.

**Accomplishments.** Designed a model rocket from scratch using whatever I had — cardboard, golf club tubing, push pins, plastic champagne flutes. It didn't need to be aerodynamic; I just wanted to test my theories and put something quick and simple together.

**Challenges.** Integration. I had all these incompatible parts and had to find a way to put them together. A fun challenge to be creative about making things work.

**Next step.** Launch the rocket, see what works and what doesn't, and decide which parts to design and 3D print for the next model.

## Second Milestone — First Launch

<div class="video-embed">
<iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/OAdHz1ghlD0" title="Rocket Milestone 2" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture" allowfullscreen></iframe>
</div>

**Overview.** Learn to launch the rocket. Then learn from what worked (and didn't) so I could iterate to a final model.

**Accomplishments.** Launched the rocket. While it failed miserably, it was clear what went wrong: bigger engine needed, better balance, less weight. I'd never worked with model rocket engines or launch controllers, so not setting anything on fire was also a huge plus.

**Challenges.** Understanding the launch components. I extensively tested the engine, starter, and controller to make sure I knew what I was doing before going out and setting things ablaze.

**Next step.** Design and 3D print all the parts for the final prototype. Make the nose cone and fins lighter and more precise.

## Final Milestone — Successful Launch + Recovery

<div class="video-embed">
<iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/ec7b5v9FoI8" title="Rocket Final Milestone" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture" allowfullscreen></iframe>
</div>

**Overview.** Successfully launched the rocket and deployed the parachute.

**Accomplishments.** Revamped almost the whole rocket. The body is shorter. New nose cone, new fins, remade engine compartment. The added precision and dropped weight is what allowed for a successful mission.

**Challenges.** Designing and printing the parts. Wasted a lot of time figuring out the right print precision — I started at 1 mm but had to move up to 2.5 mm as the right balance of precision and tolerance. It was also really hard learning to design and print a working screw mechanism for the engine bay.

**Next step.** Done with the rocket for now. Moving on to other projects, but in the future I'd like to come back and add a recovery system for the nose cone as well.

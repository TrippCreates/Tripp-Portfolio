## Overview

My first large-scale project. I built the foam RC airplane from scratch — designed it on my own, integrated foam board with RC parts, and after many iterations got it to fly. The biggest challenge came when trying to make the plane balanced and easily flyable. I was a worse pilot than I was an engineer.

Going in, I knew almost nothing about aerodynamics or physics, but that made the experience much better because I learned so much from it. The biggest takeaway: **integration is really hard.** I easily put together the electronics, and the hardware (a foam plane design I made up), but integrating the two and getting them to work in harmony took literal months. Every night I was attempting to get two opposing forces to come together.

**Overview.** First iteration of the plane built. No attempts to fly yet — the goal was to make sure all the hardware and electronics were solid.

**Accomplishments.** Designed a foam airplane that could, in theory, fly. Came up with mechanisms for the elevator and rudder out of the materials I had. Spent a lot of time researching the electronics to make sure everything was compatible and working while also trying not to set anything on fire.

**Challenges.** The electronics. I knew nothing about RC going in, so when I put it all together, nothing worked. Ended up going to a hobby store and asking for help — the people there were really nice and clearly explained how everything integrated. Soon enough my electronics were working.

**Next step.** Make solid flight attempts. I figured my design *could* fly, but some changes would be needed once I saw what didn't work in practice.

## Second Milestone — First Flight Attempts

<div class="video-embed">
<iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/ZehPrCIDyY8" title="RC Plane Milestone 2" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture" allowfullscreen></iframe>
</div>

**Overview.** Making solid attempts at flight, but still struggling to get consistently airborne.

**Accomplishments.** Hardware and electronics were done — the design was locked in. Even if I crashed and broke it, I could rebuild it and it would work. A few non-obvious changes since the first iteration:

- Reinforced the front to hold the propeller properly — combined screws, glue, and a solid piece of wood so the motor had a stable mount.
- Reorganized inside the chassis. The battery kept sliding back, unplugging itself or shifting the center of gravity mid-flight. Built a wooden-dowel system that kept the battery firmly in place.

**Challenges.** Take-offs. I could not consistently get the plane up in the air to actually practice flying. Every attempt, the plane just floated down, hit the ground, and broke its propeller.

**Next step.** Before I'd call the project done, I wanted *sustained flight*. However I got off the ground didn't matter — as long as I was in the air and in control, I'd be happy.

## Final Milestone — Sustained Flight

<div class="video-embed">
<iframe src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/_G9nHn8xFMI" title="RC Plane Final Milestone" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture" allowfullscreen></iframe>
</div>

**Overview.** Made a real attempt at flight and had sustained airtime — enough to satisfy my goal.

**Accomplishments.** Only a few changes from the prior milestone:

- Better internal compartments — easier access to plug things back in or swap parts.
- Added weight to the nose to balance the center of gravity and make the plane more aerodynamic.
- Spent time in an online flight simulator to practice in a controlled environment.

**Challenges.** Controlling the plane in air. Take-offs got more consistent, but once airborne I had no idea what to do — it took a long time to get any semblance of control.

**Next step.** Done with the plane for now. Want to move on to other projects, but in the future I'd like to come back to it — either with a better-looking and better-performing model, or by adding features like a cargo bay to this one.
